About

My interests lie in the intersection of Software Engineering, Software Security, Formal Methods, and Machine Learning. My research focuses on advancing Neuro-Symbolic AI to improve the scalability and efficiency of automated reasoning systems, while enabling AI models to develop reasoning and verification capabilities. Beyond foundational research, I design specialized Neuro-Symbolic methods to enhance software reliability, including modern AI systems, and explore verifiable code generation to make software development more trustworthy and error-resistant.

I currently lead the Hiprel Group, where I fortunately work with a number of talented and motivated students.

I did my PhD at The University of Texas at Austin, supervised by Sarfraz Khurshid. During my PhD, I have also been closely working with Kenneth McMillan and Darko Marinov.


I am looking for self-motivated PhD (Fall 2026) and internship students (all year round) who share similar research interests with me and is interested in joining our Hiprel Group! Please click here for more information. If you're interested in working with me, please fill out this form. You’re also welcome to follow up via email. While I will review all submissions, I apologize in advance if I'm unable to respond to everyone.
