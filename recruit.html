Join Us

Dr. Wenxi Wang in the CS Department at the University of Virginia invites applications for multiple student positions, including PhD and internship. The PhD student position will begin in Fall 2025, while the internship position is available year-round.


Research Interests:
Dr. Wang’s research interests lie in the intersection of Software Engineering, Software Security, Formal Methods, and Machine Learning. Her research is dedicated to enhancing the security and reliability of software systems, including AI systems. To achieve this goal, she focuses on developing and applying efficient techniques in formal methods and machine learning, as well as exploring the synergies between these two fields.


Application:
Applicants should possess a strong academic background in computer science or related fields. While experience in systems, machine learning, or formal methods is advantageous, it's your motivation for research, strong programming skills, and passion for innovation that will truly make you stand out. For more information and to express your interest, please visit Dr. Wang's webpage and consider reaching out via email (wenxiw@utexas.edu) to discuss potential opportunities.


About UVA and its CS department:
The University of Virginia, founded by the third U.S. President Thomas Jefferson in 1819, is a prestigious institution renowned for its rich history and unwavering commitment to academic excellence. In the 2024 U.S. News & World Report Rankings, UVA proudly holds the No. 5 spot among public universities and No. 24 among all U.S. universities. Our Computer Science Department fosters a collaborative and interdisciplinary research environment. With state-of-the-art facilities and dedicated faculty members, the department excels in producing innovative research with real-world impact. Notably, CSRanking has placed UVA’s CS department at No. 5 in software engineering, security, formal methods, and AI research over the past five years.


About Charlottesville:
Charlottesville offers an ideal blend of cultural richness, natural beauty, and vibrant community life. Nestled in the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains, Charlottesville is the gateway to Shenandoah National Park, offering stunning landscapes and numerous outdoor activities. The city is steeped in history and culture, with a lively arts scene, diverse dining options, and historic landmarks, including Monticello, the home of Thomas Jefferson. For those craving city life, Washington, D.C. is just a convenient 2-hour train ride away, offering numerous professional, and recreational opportunities. Join us in Charlottesville for a unique and enriching academic experience at UVA.
